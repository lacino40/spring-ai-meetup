   - The speed of light in a vacuum is constant (c ≈ 299,792 km/s) for all observers, regardless of their motion.

**3. Length Contraction:**

   - Larger masses create deeper "curvatures" in spacetime, affecting the motion of other objects.

**3. Gravitational Time Dilation:**
